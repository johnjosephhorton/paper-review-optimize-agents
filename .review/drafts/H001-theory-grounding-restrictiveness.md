# Theory-grounding requirement may be more restrictive than acknowledged

## Flagged text

> We argue that AI agents put in simulations of those novel settings offer an alternative for applying theory, requiring minimal or no modifications.

## Problem

The paper's evidence for theory-grounding comes exclusively from settings with well-established, clean theoretical models (level-k for strategic games, social preferences for allocation games). Many applied prediction problems in social science lack such clean theoretical structures. The paper should discuss the limits of the approach when theories are vague, competing, or absent, and address the practical difficulty of operationalizing theories into prompts for complex domains (labor markets, political behavior, health decisions).
