# Overclaiming Predictive Power

## Flagged text

> With \(\varepsilon = 0.2\), the strategic sample of \aisub{} is, on average, significantly more predictive than any of the benchmarks (\(p < 0.001\) for all comparisons). These differences are substantial.

## Problem

The claim that the strategic sample of AI is significantly more predictive than any benchmarks with substantial differences is strong. However, the evidence provided in the results section primarily relies on log-likelihood ratios and p-values without a comprehensive discussion of the practical significance or limitations of these findings. To support such a strong claim, the paper should include a more detailed analysis of the effect sizes, practical implications of the predictive differences, and any potential limitations or caveats. This would help ensure that the claim is not overstated and is well-supported by the evidence presented.
